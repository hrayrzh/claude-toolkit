# Interior furnishing & clearances

Furnish room-by-room, then **audit** the whole thing against walls/doors/windows. Pull exact numbers from the
**interior-design-expert** skill (`references/space-planning.md`); the essentials:

## Clearances (mm)
| Situation | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Walkway / primary circulation | 900–1200 |
| Secondary path (between furniture) | 600–900 |
| Bed — access side(s) | 600 |
| Closet / wardrobe — clear in front | 750 |
| Dining chair pull-back | 900 |
| Kitchen aisle (one cook) | ~1050 |
| In front of a toilet | ≥700 |
| Sofa ↔ TV | 2.5–3 m |
| Squeeze point (wall ↔ furniture, not a path) | 450 |

## Per-room starter kit
- **Bedroom:** bed (single ~1000×2000, double ~1600×2000 — match the room; small rooms get single beds), 1–2 nightstands,
  wardrobe, optional desk/bench. Headboard to a solid wall, not across a window if avoidable.
- **Bathroom / ensemble:** vanity+basin, toilet, and shower and/or bathtub. A "master" bath often wants **all four** (bath +
  shower + toilet + basin) — confirm. Glass shower screens; keep ≥700 in front of the toilet; door swing clear.
- **Kitchen:** L or U of base units + worktop, hob, sink (under a window if there is one), dishwasher, fridge at the end of a run
  so its door opens into the room (never blocking a pantry door), uppers (not over a window), an island with a real aisle.
- **Living:** sofa facing the TV wall (TV mounted on the wall the user points to), dining table + chairs by the glazing, optional
  window seat.
- **Utility/laundry/pantry:** washer+dryer+counter+sink; open shelving units (boards + side panels).

## Audit (do this before declaring "done")
For every piece check, against the room's inner bounds, doors and windows:
1. **No furniture inside a wall** (respect the inner face = axis ± t/2). A wardrobe poking into a wall is the #1 bug.
2. **No furniture blocking a door** (keep the opening span and a swing clearance free).
3. **Clearances met** (table above) — especially a free access side at the bed and ≥700 in front of toilets.
4. **Nothing across a passage/opening** (e.g. a hall cupboard placed across the doorway to the kitchen — keep ≥900 through).
Render a top-down per floor and walk each room in first-person to confirm.

## Area: see references/conventions.md
Net per room (inside faces), gross by axes minus notches. Report per room + floor total; areas double as a dimension sanity check.
